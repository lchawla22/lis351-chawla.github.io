My Educational History and My Views on It

Growing up, I have spent almost 10 years in the Milwaukee Public Schools District. Not only that, but I have always also been in schools that offered International Baccalaureate program. I had always been used to rigorous academic work because of IB, or so I thought. Elementary school and middle school were a breeze. 8th grade completion happened and I moved on to Ronald Reagan College Preparatory High School. At first, it was a breeze. But then COVID happened. During the virtual part of school due to COVID, I struggled a lot in the last year of high school. Teachers kept giving extreme amounts of work assuming that all students were not busy. At first I absolutely disliked it. But then, I started to try and manage my time wisely. Although it got very hard to manage school and work, I'm glad I had to experience somewhat of what college may be.

Coming to UW-Madison was such a big change. I had always known everyone in my classes and there was never more than 35 students in my classes. Having my first lecture was very intimidating. In a class of 400+ students, I felt very small. I didn't have any friends there and I didn't talk to anyone. It was scary. It was a huge culture shock and it took a while to get over it. But then I started to find friends from high school in my classes, which helped lessen my shock. I started going to a club on campus and absolutely loved it there. I started to feel like I belong on campus and I'm not an outlier. I got comfortable with the fact that I won't know everyone in my classes, which is a blessing in disguise to be honest. It gets hard remembering every name to every face. One semester later and I feel much better being on campus. It feels very familiar to me and I've gotten used to the environment. It may not have been what I was expecting but I'm glad it isn't. Coming to Madison was a great choice that I most definitely do not regret!

Places I've been to

Growing up, I didn't really see many places. Until I was about 9 years old, I lived in a small town in Illinois. The only place I had ever really ever been to was the nearby towns or Chicago. Then, my family and I moved to Wisconsin, where I've been for most of my life. Lately, I have started traveling. I recently went to New York with a friend and had such a great time. I do plan on making more trips in the future once I have more money. Some places I plan on going to are Italy, Japan and Switzerland.
