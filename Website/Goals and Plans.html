Major and Extracurriculars

Coming to UW-Madison, I wasn't really sure what I wanted to major in. This is why I still haven't declared a major. However, after taking a few classes, I do know that I would like to do something related to science. Even though I am undeclared, I think I might be interested in majoring in Biochemistry. I say this because I feel like I want the best of both worlds. Majoring in just biology or just chemistry would limit me from possible experiences that I can gain by majoring in Biochemistry.

Since it's my first year at college, I didn't join many clubs because I do want figure out the perfect balance for me. However, I have joined a student group on campus called Sikh Student Association(SSA). It doesn't have a large member population, which is one of the reasons I like it so much. It's a religious student group that focuses on inclusion and education for the Sikh population on campus and for anyone in general. It's something that has made me feel more comfortable and connected to the campus and I'm very grateful to be part of this organization.

Long Term Plans

Growing up, I always knew that I wanted to have a career in the medical field. I have attended a few shadow opportunities and I have narrowed it down to a few things. I would want to be a radiologist or a surgeon. My academic plan is to get my Bachelor's degree from UW-Madison and then do medical school. I haven't really planned where I want to go to medical school yet but I feel like I'll be able to do so by my junior year.

Personally, my long term plan is have a stable job throughout so that I don't have take out a lot of loans. Along with that, within the next 10 years, I do plan on visiting Italy and Japan as I mentioned earlier. For that, I will have to save up a lot of money, which I'm starting to do already. Overall, I plan to be at a better place than I currently am.
